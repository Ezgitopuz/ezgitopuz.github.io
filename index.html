Ezgi Topuz Buldanli

PhD Candidate · Operations Research & Logistics

Wageningen University & Research

I am a PhD researcher specializing in agent-based modeling and simulation, with a focus on behavioral dynamics and decision-making in complex systems. My modeling work spans two domains: social dynamics (opinion formation, extremism, and pluralistic ignorance) and logistics systems (freight matching platforms). My current research centers on modeling heterogeneous user decisions in freight matching platforms, examining how individual choices and interactions shape market-level outcomes.

I am passionate about leveraging computational modeling and behavioral theory to understand how decisions emerge and how system-level outcomes arise from individual interactions. Beyond logistics, my broader interest lies in building adaptive, policy-relevant simulations across socio-economic systems.

Research

PhD Project

Freight Matching as a Service (FMaaS)

My doctoral research investigates how digital freight matching platforms can be designed to operate efficiently and fairly under heterogeneous user behavior. Using agent-based modeling and behavioral operations research, I examine how individual shipper preferences and decision strategies give rise to market-level outcomes, and what platform design choices can steer these outcomes toward sustainable and commercially viable equilibria.

  • How do heterogeneous shipper preferences affect platform efficiency and fairness?
  • Under what conditions can freight matching platforms remain economically viable?
  • How can behavioral ABMs capture decision dynamics in freight markets?
